Identifying Airplanes

It's time to identify airplanes! Airplanes listed here are the basic comercial aircraft that you would most likely see at airports. Sometimes it's difficult to see which aircraft is what but usually there are differences, you just have to look close!

Boeing Vs. Airbus

The names of the aircraft depend on whether or not the plane is a Boeing or an Airbus. They are two different aircraft industries, like different candy bar brands like Hersheys or Kitkat. Boeing aircraft start with a B in front of the name like B777. And in aviation, 777 would be said as triple seven. When you see a B with numbers, you wouldn't say 'B' 777, you would say Boeing 777. It is the same as Airbus except an A is present in front of the numbers for example A350. You would also pronounce it as Airbus a three fifty. Not airbus a three five zero. For Airbus, you do say the a but for Boeing you do not say the b in pronounciation.
A Boeing aircraft typically have a sharp nose, while Airbus aircraft have a round nose in the front of the plane. However, this is not the case for some airplanes so it's very important to look at other distict features of the airplane.

Airbus Aircraft

A318

The smallest aircraft of Airbus. A twin-engine airplane with only 8 windows to the beginning of the plane to the wing. Has the distinct round nose, unique to Airbus.

A319

The second smallest aircraft of Airbus. A twin-engine airplane with only 10 windows to the beginning of the plane to the wing. Has the distinct round nose, unique to Airbus.

A320

The third smallest aircraft of Airbus. A twin-engine airplane with only 13 windows to the beginning of the plane to the wing. Has the distinct round nose, unique to Airbus. Emergency doors are right over the wing.

A321

The bigger aircraft of Airbus. A twin-engine airplane with only 16 windows to the beginning of the plane to the wing. Has the distinct round nose, unique to Airbus.

A330

An Airbus plane. A twin-engine airplane with a sharp and upright nose. Has a total of 8 rotating wheels. 4 on each side. Only has one winglet on the wing. The cockpit windows are a little bit rounded, giving it a difference from Boeing.

A340

One of the most rarest aircraft to see at an airport. Many airlines discontinue this aircraft in their fleet because of the unnessesary 4 engines, with only one row of seats. It is the only aircraft that flys today with 4 engines that only has 1 row of seats. Very few airlines still carry this aircraft because it has bad fuel effiency.

A350

Specially known for the rounded edges of the windows at the cockpit, referred to as the raccoon eyes. A twin engine jet with a total of 8 rotating wheels.

A380

The biggest comercial airplane in the world. Has 2 full double decker rows of seats with 4 engines. Only about 3 airlines carry this aircraft. This is one of the most popular airplanes to see and many people around the world go and visit these airplanes. This plane also has 22 rotating wheels.

Boeing Aircraft

B737

Has a very sharp nose that Boeings have. The winglet at the very edge of the wing has one winglet going up and then a smaller one below it. It's also a twin engine jet with only 3 tires in total.

B747

The biggest airplane Boeing has ever made. Has 4 engines with a second row with only a couple seats. The second floor does not go all the way to the fin unlike the a380. The Boeing 747 has a total of 18 wheels. The 747 has a special nickname and they are always known as "the Queen of the Skies".

B757

Another Boeing Aircraft. Has a steep but also round nose like Airbus and a unique body in general. However, this aircraft has a total of 9 wheels, 8 which are rotating unlike the 737 where no rotating wheels are present. This plane sometimes has or doesn't have winglets.

B767

The not as popular Boeing aircraft. The cockpit windows are shapped a little bit differently than a normal boeing. They are just a tiny bit rounder. This plane also sometimes has or doesn't have winglets. The nose isn't as sharp with a gradual decline.

B777

The second largest aircraft of Boeing, following the 747. Has a total of 14 tires. Easy distinguishable by the extra strip of tail below the rudder in the back of the airplane.

B787

Another twin engine jet with an interesting coned nose like a birds beak. Has ridges triangles around the engine. Has 8 rotating tires.
