Aviation Vocabulary

Wondering about aviation vocabulary? Not to worry! Many pilots, ATC, and plane spotters use specific aviation vocabulary. For example Livery, which is most commonly used for plane spotters. It's bascially the word for saying that a plane is decorated and has a special paint job. A good example of a plane livery is the plane in the first image of the Identifying Airplanes page. Something special among Pilots and ATC is that they don't say letters. For T they would say Tango. They use this vocabulary for different taxiways or to address plane call signs over the radio. Many planespotters use Live ATC, and they listen to what the ATC says to the Pilots! It's a very useful and interesting tool in plane spotting.
